Youth Leadership and Active Citizenship

The Youth Leadership and Active Citizenship programme aims at providing youth, of different socio-economic backgrounds, with the support and platforms to participate in leadership initiatives and be active members of the Rwandan society. Through youth forums and training, the youth is encouraged to discuss and identify common solutions, while at the same time, being oriented towards personal development.

Face of Imbuto
Emmanuel Niyonsenga (KEZEM)

My name is Emmanuel Niyonsenga and I am the CEO and Founder of KEZEM Fashion House. Raised by a single mother, I faced many challenges growing up, as my father was killed in the 1994 Genocide against the Tutsi before I was even born. Despite these hardships, I was fortunate to continue my education through university.

Even with a bachelor’s degree in a different field, I chose to follow
my true passion—fashion. With the support of Imbuto Foundation through the Art-Rwanda Ubuhanzi programme, I launched KEZEM Fashion House in 2020. Today,
our business proudly employs 15 permanent and 5 casual workers.

Attending Imbuto Foundation’s mentorship programmes and youth forums has significantly enhanced my leadership and management skills, transforming me into a more active and engaged citizen. Through these experiences, I have gained the confidence and knowledge to lead effectively and contribute meaningfully to my community. I urge my fellow youth to take advantage of similar opportunities to grow, learn, and become leaders who drive positive change in their communities.
